Complete Lawn Care Guide for Maryland

Maryland’s climate has distinct seasons that directly affect lawn health. To keep your lawn green, strong, and weed-free, you need a maintenance routine tailored to each season. This guide explains step-by-step how to care for your lawn throughout the year using professional techniques we apply at Laguna Lawn Service.

1. Spring: The Lawn Awakens

Spring is the most important season. This is when you repair winter damage and prepare the soil for strong growth.

  • Core Aeration:
    Winter compacts the soil, limiting airflow and water penetration. Aeration opens the soil so oxygen, water, and nutrients reach the roots more effectively.
  • Dethatching:
    Thatch is a layer of dead organic material that blocks airflow and encourages fungus. Removing it improves water absorption and overall lawn health.
  • Early Fertilization:
    A nitrogen-rich fertilizer boosts early growth. Always follow recommended application rates.
  • Pre-emergent Weed Control:
    Applying pre-emergent products prevents weeds from sprouting, which is far more effective than removing them later.

2. Summer: Heat Protection

Maryland summers can be hot and humid. Without proper watering and mowing, lawns weaken and become vulnerable to weeds and insects.

  • Deep, Infrequent Watering:
    Water 2–3 times per week early in the morning. Deep watering encourages strong root growth.
  • Higher Mowing Height:
    Keep grass between 3.5 and 4 inches to protect roots from heat and reduce moisture loss.
  • Insect Monitoring:
    Look for signs of chinch bugs, grubs, and other pests that damage turf.

3. Fall: Repair and Strengthen

Fall is the best season for repairing damage and preparing the lawn for winter.

  • Overseeding:
    Adding new seed fills thin areas and improves lawn density.
  • Fall Fertilization:
    Fertilizers with phosphorus and potassium strengthen roots and prepare the lawn for cold weather.
  • Leaf Removal:
    Leaves block sunlight and trap moisture, creating fungus. Remove them regularly.

4. Winter: Rest and Preparation

  • Avoid heavy objects: They can damage dormant turf.
  • Limit foot traffic: Frozen grass breaks easily.
  • Prepare equipment: Sharpen blades and service your mower for spring.
Mowing Tips

How to Mow Your Lawn Correctly for a Healthy Yard

Mowing may seem simple, but doing it incorrectly can weaken your lawn and cause long-term damage. With the right height, frequency, and technique, you can achieve a professional cut.

1. Correct Mowing Height

  • Spring: 3 inches
  • Summer: 3.5–4 inches
  • Fall: 3 inches

2. Ideal Mowing Frequency

  • Spring: every 5–7 days
  • Summer: every 7–10 days
  • Fall: every 5–7 days

3. Keep Blades Sharp

Dull blades tear grass instead of cutting it, causing brown tips and increasing disease risk.

4. Change Mowing Patterns

Alternate directions weekly to prevent soil compaction and uneven growth.

5. Avoid Mowing Wet Grass

Wet grass clumps, causes uneven cuts, and stresses your mower.

6. Use Mulching

Mulched clippings return natural nitrogen to the soil and help retain moisture.

Fertilization

Common Lawn Fertilizing Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

Fertilizing is essential for lawn health, but mistakes can burn grass or encourage weeds. Here are the most common errors and how to avoid them.

1. Using Too Much Fertilizer

Excess fertilizer burns grass and pollutes soil. Always follow product instructions.

2. Incorrect Measuring

Use a calibrated spreader to ensure even distribution.

3. Fertilizing at the Wrong Time

  • Spring: growth boost
  • Summer: maintenance
  • Fall: root strengthening
  • Winter: do not fertilize

4. Not Watering After Fertilizing

Water activates nutrients and prevents leaf burn.

5. Using the Wrong Fertilizer

  • Nitrogen (N): green growth
  • Phosphorus (P): root strength
  • Potassium (K): stress resistance
